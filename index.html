About me:
I am an Assistant Professor at the Luxembourg Centre for Logistics and Supply Chain Management, University of Luxembourg. My research focuses on optimization under uncertainty with applications in economics, policy and mechanism design. Prior to joining the University of Luxembourg, I received a Ph.D. in Management of Technology from École Polytechnique Fédérale de Lausanne (EPFL) in 2020, where I worked with Daniel Kuhn. I hold a B.Sc. degree and an M.Sc. degree in Industrial Engineering from Bilkent University.

Key areas of interest:
Optimization under uncertainty: (distributionally) robust optimization, stochastic and dynamic programming
Policy and mechanism design: resource allocation problems, auctions, pricing, societal problems, fairness, equity
